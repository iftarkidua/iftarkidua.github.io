The Sacred Iftar Dua: A Comprehensive Guide

The Authentic Iftar Dua

اللَّهُمَّ إِنِّي لَكَ صُمْتُ وَبِكَ آمَنْتُ وَعَلَيْكَ تَوَكَّلْتُ وَعَلَى رِزْقِكَ أَفْطَرْتُ

Transliteration

"Allahumma inni laka sumtu wa bika aamantu wa alayka tawakkaltu wa ala rizq-ika aftartu"

English Translation

"O Allah! I fasted for You, and I believe in You, and I put my trust in You, and I break my fast with Your sustenance."

Spiritual Significance

This profound dua encapsulates four essential pillars of faith:

  • Devotional Commitment: "I fasted for You" - Affirmation of worship purpose
  • Faith Declaration: "I believe in You" - Reinforcement of Islamic creed
  • Divine Reliance: "I put my trust in You" - Expression of Tawakkul
  • Grateful Acknowledgement: "With Your sustenance" - Recognition of Allah's provision

Historical Context

This dua finds its roots in the Sunnah of Prophet Muhammad (PBUH), documented in various authentic Hadith collections including Abu Dawood and Tirmidhi. Scholars emphasize its comprehensive nature, combining both physical and spiritual dimensions of fasting.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q1: When should I recite this dua?

Recite immediately before breaking your fast, preferably while still fasting and holding the first morsel/drink.

Q2: Can I add personal supplications?

Absolutely. After reciting the traditional dua, you may add personal prayers in any language.

Q3: Is there a preferred hand position?

While no specific position is mandated, many raise their hands in the classical dua position (palms upward).
